üîπ Rules of Verbal Communication

Clarity: Speak clearly; avoid unnecessary jargon.

Tone: Match tone to context; avoid sounding rude.

Pace & Volume: Moderate speed and volume; pause at key points.

Active Listening: Listen fully; don't interrupt.

Relevance: Stay on topic; be concise.

Respect: Use polite words and value others' time.

In short: Be clear, respectful, and adaptable.

Correct Usage of Also, As well and Too

Also

Formal: Usually before main verb or at beginning. Example: "I can code in Python. Also, I am learning Java."

As well

Informal/Polite: Usually at sentence end. Example: "She brought her laptop as well."

Too

Casual: Usually at sentence end. Example: "I like pizza too."

Quick Tips

  • Use also in mid-sentence or formal writing.
  • Use too at the end in casual speech.
  • Use as well at the end when polite/neutral.
