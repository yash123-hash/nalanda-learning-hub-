The Entrepreneur as a Problem Solver

Problem Solving

We often face challenges but rush to solutions without carefully analyzing the real problem. A good problem-solving approach is to first identify the root issue, then test whether the solution is new, useful, and practical. Problems usually have multiple causes, such as:

  • The need for improvement (better, faster, easier ways)
  • Changes in the world impacting industries, products, or services
  • Market trends shaped by location, demographics, or customer psychology

A food delivery startup notices declining orders. Instead of immediately lowering prices (solution-jumping), they analyze the problem:

  • Customers want faster deliveries (need for improvement).
  • More people are cooking at home due to a health trend (world change).
  • Young customers in their city prefer eco-friendly packaging (market trend).

By identifying these root causes, the company could design better solutionsâ€”like faster delivery zones, healthy meal options, and sustainable packagingâ€”rather than just cutting prices.

Steps to Solving Problems

Being an entrepreneur means you must be good at solving problems. Here is a step-by-step approach on how to solve problems. Practice these steps as you begin to think about a new entrepreneurial venture.

Problem-Solving: A Step-by-Step Approach

Identify the problem

There is no better starting point than defining what it is that needs to be fixed.

Determine the root cause

Once you have identified what your problem is, you need to figure out why it is.

  • What is behind it?
  • What is causing it?
  • Can it be quantified or qualified?
  • What is going on at a core level?

Find multiple solutions

Being a good problem-solver means thinking innovatively, and that means thinking outside the box. Do not settle for the first solution you find. Push the boat out. Find as many alternative solutions as you can. And then find some more.

Find the solutions that work best

Easier said than done? Not necessarily. Go about it logically. Answer these questions:

  • Is it technically viable?
  • Is it scalable?
  • Do you have the resources?
  • What are the risks? Can they be managed?
  • Does your solution benefit as many people as possible?
  • Can it be measured? How will you measure it?

Plan and implement your solutions

Give this part plenty of thought, too. Build a really tight plan to execute your solution. You will need to cover who, what, when, and how you will implement your plan.

Measure the success of your solutions

How does it measure against your goals? Have you met your objectives? Have you stayed within budget? Is the work complete? Can you see a measurable outcome?

Remember that all problems are simply puzzles waiting to be solved. Practice using these six steps to build your problem-solving acumen. These are the steps to being a problem solver.

Woman-Owned Businesses

Women entrepreneurs have increased over the years. According to the GEM 2021/2022 Report:

  • 2 in 5 early-stage businesses are started by women.
  • Almost half of women entrepreneurs work in Wholesale/Retail (e.g., opening clothing stores, grocery shops, or online marketplaces).
